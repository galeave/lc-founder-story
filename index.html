About Me

I'm Eleza, a product leader who has spent over 20 years building software, apps, and digital experiences. (You can explore my professional background on LinkedIn.)

Alongside my career in tech, I've spent much of my life practicing martial arts:

  • Black belt in Goju Ryu Karate
  • Several years of kickboxing
  • Early experience in BJJ

Training didn't make me a professional fighter, but it shaped how I see the sport - the discipline, repetition, conditioning, injuries, mental toughness, and quiet resilience that fighters live with every single day.

What I'm Building (and Why)

The deeper I train and follow combat sports, the more one reality stands out:

Fighters are being pushed to do more - far beyond training and competing.

To increase their earning potential, fighters today are expected to:

  • build personal brands
  • produce constant content
  • sell merch
  • manage multiple platforms
  • maintain engagement to keep algorithms happy

All of this sits on top of the demands of training, recovering, and staying competitive.

At the same time, die-hard fight fans want to support fighters - especially those who leave everything inside the cage but don't receive the financial upside.

Yet there's still no simple, trusted, direct way for fans to financially support a fighter without friction or noise.

This gap is the reason LevelChange exists.

LevelChange Mission

I'm building LevelChange to give fighters and fans a clean, direct line to each other.

LevelChange makes support simple:
